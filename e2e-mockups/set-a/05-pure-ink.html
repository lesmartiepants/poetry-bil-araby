LABID IBN RABI'AH · MU'ALLAQA · LINE 01
عَفَتِ الدِيارُ مَحَلُّها فَمُقامُها
بِمِنىً تَأَبَّدَ غَولُها فَرِجامُها
The dwellings are effaced, their brief encampments and long stays,
At Mina — overgrown now are Ghawl and Rijam.
transliteration: ʿafati d-diyāru maḥalluhā fa-muqāmuhā

Labid's opening atlal (ruins) passage is considered the finest example of the classical qasida structure. The abandoned campsite becomes a canvas for memory and loss.
