Imru al-Qais • The Mu'allaqa
قِفا نَبكِ مِن ذِكرى حَبيبٍ وَمَنزِلِ
بِسِقطِ اللِوى بَينَ الدَخولِ فَحَومَلِ
Stop and weep for the memory of a beloved and a dwelling,
At the curve of the dune between al-Dakhul and Hawmal.
✨

The opening of the Mu'allaqa — the most famous opening line in Arabic poetry. Imru al-Qais commands his companions to stop at the ruins of his beloved's campsite, beginning the qasida tradition.
