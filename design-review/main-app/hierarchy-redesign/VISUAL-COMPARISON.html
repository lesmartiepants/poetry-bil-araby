Main App Hierarchy

Design Review — Three visual alternatives to fix UI/UX hierarchy issues

Identified Issues

6 Problems Found

  • Hierarchy Brand header renders at clamp(3rem, 6vw, 4.5rem) — 48–72px — while the poet name (the primary content ID) is at clamp(1.1rem, 3vw, 1.6rem). The app identity is louder than the content.
  • Contrast English translations at opacity-40 produce approximately 3.5:1 contrast on #0c0c0e — below WCAG AA (4.5:1 minimum for body text).
  • Motion slide-in-from-bottom-8 duration-1000 on every poem load is a full-second, 32px-travel slide — aggressive and distracting. Layout shifts visible as height animates.
  • Motion Header scroll collapse uses scale() transform with mismatched durations on transform (0.4s) vs opacity (0.3s), producing a clunky, non-synchronized shrink effect.
  • Clutter SVG corner frame brackets (8 line elements + 4 circle dots in a 550×120 viewBox) add visual noise around the poem title with no semantic meaning.
  • Spacing Verse gap of gap-5 (1.25rem) is tight for poetry — reading rhythm requires more breathing room between stanzas.

Option 1 Refined Current

Open Full Preview →
Live preview — hover to interact

Design Direction

Keeps the dark mystical aesthetic intact. Corrects every broken ratio without changing the fundamental visual language. The safest migration path.

Key Changes

  • Brand header: clamp(3rem, 6vw, 4.5rem) 1.2rem static — shrunk ~60%, still centered
  • Poet name: clamp(1.1rem, 3vw, 1.6rem) clamp(1.8rem, 4vw, 2.5rem) — promoted, gold, bold
  • Poem title: clamp(1.3rem, 3.5vw, 2rem) clamp(1.5rem, 3vw, 2rem) — cleaner range
  • SVG frame brackets removed — replaced with a 60px gold <hr> at opacity 0.4
  • English translations: opacity 0.4 opacity 0.6 — WCAG AA compliant
  • Verse entry: slide-in-from-bottom-8 1s fadeIn 0.6s ease-out
  • Verse gap: gap-5 (1.25rem) 2.5rem
  • Verses: clamp(1.25rem, 2vw, 1.5rem) clamp(1.4rem, 2.5vw, 1.8rem) + leading 2.4

Best For

Teams wanting minimal risk migration. All six issues resolved while preserving existing visual identity.

Option 2 Poet-First Layout

Open Full Preview →
Live preview — hover to interact

Design Direction

Radically rebalances the information architecture. Brand becomes an ambient fixture; the poet becomes the true protagonist of the UI. Strong editorial character — closest to how literary publications present poetry.

Key Changes

  • Brand: 48–72px centered 14px top-left corner, 50% opacity — brand stays, doesn't compete
  • Poet name: clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3rem) — dominant visual anchor, gold glow
  • Poem title: clamp(1.4rem, 3vw, 1.8rem) beneath poet, clearly secondary
  • Verse entry: staggered fadeUp, 150ms per verse — builds narrative tension on load
  • Verse gap: 3rem — generous stanza breathing room
  • No SVG frame, no center header chrome
  • Translations: opacity 0.6 — WCAG AA pass

Best For

Maximum content hierarchy clarity. Users who come for specific poets. Most distinctive design direction — strongest editorial statement.

Option 3 Ambient / Full Immersion

Open Full Preview →
Live preview — hover to interact

Design Direction

Zero top chrome. Brand dissolves into ambient background. The poem and its poet are the entire UI. Reading-app feeling — closer to Instapaper or iBooks than a social platform. Maximum focus.

Key Changes

  • Top header: fixed 64px header removed entirely
  • Brand: 11px, 30% opacity, bottom-left fixed — exists but whispers
  • Poet name: true <h1>, clamp(2.2rem, 5vw, 3.2rem) — owns the full viewport top
  • Separator: 4px dot, 25% opacity — minimal to the point of disappearing
  • Verse entry: fadeUp 8px over 0.5s, staggered
  • Translations: opacity 0.6 — WCAG AA pass
  • Control bar border: rgba(255,255,255,0.05) — barely there

Best For

Deep reading sessions. Mobile-first users. Audiences who treat the app as a contemplative, literary experience rather than a discovery platform.

Recommendations

Best Overall: Option 2 (Poet-First) — Solves all six issues while making the strongest editorial statement. The brand-to-corner treatment is increasingly standard in literary apps, and the staggered verse entry adds meaningful delight without being gratuitous.
Lowest Risk: Option 1 (Refined Current) — If there's brand-equity concern about shrinking the header further, Option 1 is the safest path. All accessibility issues are fixed, motion is calmed, hierarchy is corrected.
Best for Mobile / Deep Reading: Option 3 (Ambient) — Removing the header entirely gives the poem full viewport ownership on small screens. Consider this if analytics show primary usage is mobile and session depth (poems per session) is a key metric.
Quick Win (any option): Fix translations from opacity-40 to opacity-60 immediately — this is a WCAG AA failure and takes 30 seconds in src/app.jsx:1175.
