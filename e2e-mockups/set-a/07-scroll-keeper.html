Tarafa ibn al-'Abd • Mu'allaqa
لِخَولَةَ أَطلالٌ بِبُرقَةِ ثَهمَدِ
تَلوحُ كَباقي الوَشمِ في ظاهِرِ اليَدِ
Khawla has traces at the stony ground of Thahmad,
appearing like the remnants of a tattoo on the back of a hand.
▶ Listen♡ Save↺ NextAa Size

Tarafa, the youngest of the Mu'allaqa poets, opens with one of Arabic poetry's most beautiful similes — comparing faded ruins to a tattoo on the hand, blending permanence and impermanence.
