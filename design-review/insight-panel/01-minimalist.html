أحبُّكِ جِدّاً

Nizar Qabbani · I Love You Intensely

أُحِبُّكِ جِدًّا

أُحِبُّكِ جِدًّا

وَأَعْرِفُ أَنَّ الطَّرِيقَ إِلَى الْمُسْتَحِيلِ طَوِيلٌ

وَلٰكِنْ أُحِبُّكِ جِدًّا

فَمَاذَا أَفْعَلُ؟

Translation

I love you so much / I love you so much / And I know that the road to the impossible is long / But I love you so much / So what can I do?

The Depth

Nizar Qabbani's brevity here is disarming. The repetition of "I love you so much" creates a hypnotic rhythm, emphasizing both certainty and helplessness. The phrase "the road to the impossible" reveals the poem's central tension -- love as simultaneously inevitable and unattainable.

The final question -- "So what can I do?" -- is rhetorical yet devastating. It is not a plea for advice but a surrender to the overwhelming force of desire. Qabbani strips away metaphor, leaving only the raw vulnerability of a lover who knows they are pursuing the impossible but cannot stop.

The Author

Nizar Qabbani (1923-1998) is often called the poet of love and revolution. Born in Damascus, he scandalized Arab society with his frank treatment of romantic and erotic themes, breaking centuries of poetic convention. His work challenged patriarchal norms while celebrating feminine beauty and agency.

Qabbani's directness -- evident in this poem -- was revolutionary. He wrote in accessible, conversational Arabic rather than ornate classical forms, making poetry a vehicle for everyday emotion. His later work grew more political, but his love poems remain his most enduring legacy.
